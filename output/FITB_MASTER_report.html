1 · Executive summary

Fifth Third is a newly-enlarged super-regional (post-Comerica, ~$288B assets, 9th-largest US bank) built on a consumer franchise that funds the bank and drives growth (consumer interest income 11% CAGR, ~60% volume-driven; CSBB = ~⅔ of NII). We value it with 14 independent methods spanning intrinsic, relative, structural, forecast-driven, expectations and M&A lenses. 3 · Warranted-multiple regression (P/TBV ~ ROTCE)

The classic bank cross-sectional test: better-returning banks earn a higher premium to tangible book. Peers price roughly 9.7x of P/TBV per 1.0 of ROTCE. At FITB's 12.6% ROTCE the regression warrants ~1.68x P/TBV (≈ $55); FITB actually trades at 1.64x.

4 · Reverse DCF — what's priced in

Inverting the bank identity at today's price (1.77× tangible book, 12.6% ROTCE, 8.8% cost of equity) implies the market is pricing ~3.9% perpetual growth. Our base case assumes 4.0% — so at the current price the stock is discounting LESS growth than our base case (modestly cheap). Fair value at our base-case growth would be ~$58.

5 · Capital-return yield

Total shareholder yield ≈ 4.3% (dividend 2.8% + buyback ~1.5%), against an earnings yield of 5.1%. A healthy, well-covered capital return supports the shares while synergies build.
