1 · Investment Thesis

Fifth Third has just transformed itself: the all-stock acquisition of Comerica ($12.7B, closed 2026-02-01) makes it the 9th largest U.S. bank with roughly $288B of assets. The market is looking through a Q1-26 EPS trough of $0.15 — depressed by $635M of one-off merger charges — toward a franchise with a sub-57% efficiency ratio, double-digit ROTCE, a low-72% loan-to-core-deposit ratio, and $850M of targeted cost synergies. The core question for the stock: does management capture those synergies and lift combined ROTCE toward ~14%+ without a credit or integration accident? If yes, the shares are worth meaningfully more than tangible book. Our blended fair value of $55.42 implies -4.3% vs the current $57.89.

2 · How Fifth Third makes money

The bank reports three segments — Commercial Banking, Consumer & Small Business Banking, Wealth & Asset Management. Roughly two-thirds of revenue is net interest income (spread on $178B of loans funded by $234B of deposits; Q1-26 NIM 3.30%), and the rest is fee income — a genuinely diversified, capital-light engine:

8 · Risks & catalysts

Catalysts: hitting the $850M synergy run-rate by Q4-26; NIM expansion if the Fed cuts (guide assumes none); buybacks resuming once the deal's capital is digested; WAM/payments fee momentum.
Risks: integration missteps on the largest deal in company history; commercial-real-estate / C&I credit if the economy rolls over (NCOs are low now at 0bps but that is cyclical); deposit competition compressing NIM; regulatory review of a top-10 bank; goodwill from the deal makes tangible book optically thinner.
