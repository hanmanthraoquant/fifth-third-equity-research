1 · The full picture in one paragraph

Fifth Third enters 2026 as a newly-enlarged super-regional (post-Comerica, ~$288B assets) with a consumer franchise that is both the funding engine and the growth engine: consumer interest income compounded 11%/yr and that growth was ~60% volume-driven (balance sheet growth, led by auto & solar), not just higher rates. On top of that, deposit costs are now falling off the rate peak and management is targeting $850M of cost synergies. Feed those drivers through a standard earnings build and EPS recovers from a merger-charge trough toward $3.68 (2026E) → $4.80 (2028E), a 14% CAGR. Putting the intrinsic valuation and the forward-multiple target together gives a base-case 12-month target of $58 (+0.7% vs $57.89).

4 · What has to go right (and wrong)

Bull levers: synergies land on schedule (efficiency to low-50s), auto/home-equity & Southeast households keep compounding, Fed cuts widen NIM, buybacks resume. Bear risks: integration slips, consumer credit normalizes faster (NCOs already rising 33→62bps), deposit competition re-tightens funding costs, CRE/C&I stress. The forecast bakes these into the efficiency, NCO and growth drivers per scenario.
